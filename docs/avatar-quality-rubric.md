# Avatar Quality Rubric

## Scoring Standard

Every avatar is scored against the benchmark avatar (Jamal Reimer = 100%) across 8 dimensions.
**Pass threshold: 80/100.** Below 80 requires web research and improvement.

## Dimensions

| # | Dimension | Weight | 9-10 (Excellent) | 6-8 (Good) | 3-5 (Needs Work) | 1-2 (Unacceptable) |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------------|------------|-------------------|--------------------|
| 1 | **Biographical Accuracy** | 10% | Full career arc with dates, companies, roles, specific numbers. All verifiable from 3+ independent sources. | Career arc present with most details verifiable. Minor gaps. | Generic bio without dates or specific accomplishments. Some claims unverified. | "Thought leader in sales." No specific career facts. |
| 2 | **Core Frameworks** | 20% | 7+ distinct frameworks, each with specific steps/components, sourced to published work, and actionable without reading the source. | 4-6 frameworks, most sourced and actionable. Some lack specificity. | 2-3 frameworks, partially sourced. Generic application guidance. | Frameworks are vague principles ("be customer-centric") not structured methodologies. |
| 3 | **Framework Completeness** | 15% | All major published frameworks included. No significant gaps. If the person is known for X, X is in the file. | Most major frameworks included. 1-2 notable gaps. | Several major frameworks missing. File feels incomplete. | Only the most famous framework included. Everything else missing. |
| 4 | **Practical Application** | 15% | Every framework includes step-by-step application guidance. Could be used in a real deal/account/meeting tomorrow. | Most frameworks are actionable. Some require interpretation. | Frameworks described but application unclear. "Use this to improve discovery." | Purely theoretical. No execution guidance. |
| 5 | **Voice & Authenticity** | 10% | Direct quotes woven throughout. Communication style is specific (sentence patterns, energy, pet phrases). Reads like the real person. | Voice is recognisable but occasionally generic. Some direct quotes. | Voice is a generic advisor wearing the person's name. | Indistinguishable from any other avatar. |
| 6 | **Signature Questions** | 10% | 8+ questions that are distinctly theirs. Removing the name, an expert could identify who wrote them. | 6-8 questions, mostly distinctive. Some could be anyone. | 4-5 generic sales questions with the person's name attached. | Questions are generic ("What's the decision process?") and not tied to specific frameworks. |
| 7 | **Council Integration** | 10% | 4+ debate pairings with bidirectional tension explained. Operator context section with specific connections to real priorities. | 3-4 pairings with reasonable tensions. Operator context present but general. | 1-2 pairings, tensions vaguely described. Weak operator connection. | No pairings. No operator context. Avatar is isolated. |
| 8 | **Supporting Tools** | 10% | Dedicated templates, trackers, reference cards, or decision matrices (like Jamal Reimer's 7-file system). | Some supporting structure (checklists, quick references). | No supporting tools. | No supporting tools. |

## Scoring Process

1. Read the avatar file completely
2. Score each dimension 1-10 using the descriptors above
3. Multiply each score by its weight
4. Sum for total score (out of 100)
5. If total < 80: identify weakest dimensions and research to improve
