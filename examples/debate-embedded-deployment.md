# Example: Council Debate — Embedded Deployment Model
*Source: Eval #28 — 4/4 assertions passed*

## What Makes This a Good Output

- 6 avatars convened (Reimer, van der Kooij, McMahon, Roberge, Adamson, Norton)
- 5+ genuine disagreements — not variations of agreement
- 3 direct named contradictions:
  - van der Kooij vs Reimer on moat/trap
  - McMahon vs Adamson on qualification
  - Norton vs Adamson on category creation
- New tension surfaced that wasn't in any prior analysis (Customer Readiness Risk)
- 3 unresolved questions stated explicitly — no forced consensus
- The debate format works because avatars commit to positions, not hedged observations

## Anti-Pattern This Avoids

Bad council debates produce 5 avatars who all agree from different angles, then
synthesise into a single recommendation. That's a council session wearing a debate
costume. Real debates require genuine intellectual friction — avatars must be willing
to say "I disagree with [Name] because [specific logic]."
