## Deal Execution & Qualification

**John McMahon** — Enterprise deal inspection. Qualification over assumption.
- **MEDDPICC Deep Qualification:** Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Paper Process, Implicate Pain, Champion, Competition. Apply when: any deal >$50K needs qualification pressure-test. Ask: "Who loses if this deal doesn't happen?"
- **Champion Testing:** Champions are built, not found. Test: can they sell when you're not in the room? Will they give you access to the EB? Apply when: pipeline depends on a single contact.
- **Paper Process Discipline:** At 120 days to close, map who signs, in what order, what documentation, what legal review. If you can't map it, it's at risk.
- **Deal Inspection Framework:** Stage regression, timeline slippage, single-threaded risk, ghost opportunities. Apply when: forecast accuracy is low.

**Jamal Reimer** — Mega-deal architecture. Deals >$250K need design, not selling.
- **Two Mountain Model:** Mountain 1 = your sales process. Mountain 2 = their buying process. Both must be climbed simultaneously. If you're only climbing Mountain 1, you're alone.
- **Pipeline Flywheel:** Current deals generate future pipeline through relationship expansion. Every deal is both a close AND a source. Apply when: pipeline feels thin despite activity.
- **Mega-Deal Architecture:** Executive alignment, multi-stakeholder orchestration, deal variable management, staged commitments. Apply when: deal ACV >$250K or involves 5+ stakeholders.
- **Deal Variable Matrix:** Map all variables (price, terms, scope, timeline, risk) and trade them deliberately. Never concede without gaining.

**Chris Voss** — Negotiation through emotional intelligence, not leverage.
- **Tactical Empathy:** Understand counterpart's feelings/mindset — intelligence gathering, not sympathy. Apply when: procurement, churn saves, high-stakes conversations.
- **Mirroring + Labelling:** Mirror last 1-3 words (rapport). Label emotions: "It seems like..." (defuses). Apply when: buyer is emotional or revealing.
- **Calibrated Questions:** "How" and "What" questions giving counterpart illusion of control. Apply when: price/terms pressure.
- **Three Types of Yes:** Commitment (real), Confirmation (fact), Counterfeit (escape). Most reported "yeses" are counterfeit.
- **Black Swan Theory:** 3-5 unknown unknowns per negotiation. Deal-changers hide in undiscovered information.

**Brent Adamson** — Challenge the buyer's thinking to earn the right to sell.
- **Challenger Methodology (Teach, Tailor, Take Control):** Lead with insight the buyer doesn't have. Tailor to their specific context. Take control of the commercial conversation. Apply when: buyer is comparing you to commoditised alternatives.
- **Buyer Consensus Problem:** Average B2B deal has 6.8 decision-makers. More stakeholders = more "no decision." Apply when: deal stalls despite champion enthusiasm.
- **JOLT Effect (Buyer Indecision):** Judge indecision level, Offer recommendation, Limit exploration, Take risk off the table. Apply when: buyer says "we need more time" repeatedly.

## Pipeline & Prospecting

**Jeb Blount** — Pipeline solves everything. Discipline over motivation.
- **Fanatical Prospecting Doctrine:** Prospect every day. The 30-Day Rule: pipeline you generate today closes 90 days from now. Neglect today = empty pipeline in 90 days. Apply when: pipeline coverage below 3.5x.
- **Five Disciplines of Ultra-High Performance:** Manage emotions, manage pipeline, manage time, manage territory, manage sales process. Apply when: rep is inconsistent.
- **Objections Framework (Staying in the Moment):** Ledge → Disrupt → Ask. Don't answer objections — disrupt the pattern then ask a question. Apply when: objection handling training.
- **Sales EQ:** Emotional control beats IQ in sales. Manage disruptive emotions (fear, desperation, attachment). Apply when: AM showing neediness or desperation.
- **30-Day Blitz:** Intensive prospecting sprint. 3,000 touches in 30 days across phone, email, social. Apply when: pipeline emergency.

**Aaron Ross** — Specialise roles, systematise outbound, make pipeline predictable.
- **Four Core Sales Roles:** SDR (outbound), AE (closing), AM (expansion), CSM (retention). Mixing roles = dilution. Apply when: designing team structure.
- **Seeds, Nets, Spears:** Seeds = referrals (slow, high quality). Nets = marketing (medium). Spears = outbound (fast, controllable). Apply when: pipeline source mix is wrong.
- **Cold Calling 2.0:** Target → Research → Personalise → Multi-touch. Not cold. Warm-targeted. Apply when: outbound strategy design.

**Josh Braun** — Remove pressure. Let curiosity drive discovery.
- **Permission-Based Prospecting:** Ask permission before pitching. "Would it be a terrible idea if..." Removes reactance. Apply when: outbound messaging feels pushy.
- **Discovery Through Curiosity:** Ask questions you genuinely don't know the answer to. If you already know, it's a leading question. Apply when: discovery calls feel scripted.
- **Detachment Philosophy:** The less attached to the outcome, the better the conversation. Apply when: AM is chasing or over-following-up.

**Steli Efti** — Speed kills doubt. Follow up until you get a yes or a no.
- **Follow-Up Formula:** Follow up until you get a definitive answer. No response ≠ no. Apply when: deals going dark, no response patterns.
- **Founder-Led Sales (5-Stage Transition):** Founder sells → hires first rep → builds process → hires manager → scales. Apply when: new motion design.
- **Speed as Strategy:** The fastest company wins. Respond in minutes, not days. Apply when: deal velocity is low.
- **Closing Philosophy:** Ask for the close. Then shut up. Silence is a tool. Apply when: AMs avoid asking for the business.

## Strategy & Operations

**Jacco van der Kooij** — Revenue is architecture, not heroics.
- **Bow-Tie Revenue Model:** Entire customer journey from awareness → acquisition → adoption → expansion → advocacy. Most companies only measure the left side. Apply when: GTM model design, NRR diagnosis.
- **SPICED Discovery:** Situation, Pain, Impact, Critical Event, Decision. Sequential diagnostic. Apply when: discovery call preparation, deal qualification layer.
- **Revenue Architecture Principles:** Segment → specialise → standardise → scale. In that order. Apply when: org design, team structuring.

**David Skok** — Unit economics are the truth. Everything else is narrative.
- **SaaS Metrics Dashboard:** LTV:CAC (>3x), CAC Payback (<18 months), NRR (>120% = almost nothing else matters), Gross Margin. Apply when: business case evaluation, pricing decisions.
- **Negative Churn:** Expansion revenue from existing customers exceeds churned revenue. The holy grail. Apply when: retention/expansion strategy.
- **Growth Efficiency:** Rule of 40 (growth rate + profit margin > 40%). Apply when: evaluating company health or investment decisions.

**Peter Drucker** — Effectiveness over efficiency. Contribution over activity.
- **Five Practices of Effective Executives:** Know thy time, focus on contribution, build on strengths, concentrate on the few (not the many), make effective decisions. Apply when: personal effectiveness, time management, priority setting.
- **Abandonment Principle:** Systematically abandon what no longer works. "If we weren't already doing this, would we start now?" Apply when: resource allocation, initiative triage.

**Donella Meadows** — Systems, not symptoms. Leverage points, not brute force.
- **12 Leverage Points:** Most powerful: change the mindset out of which the system arises. Least powerful: adjust parameters (quotas, targets). Apply when: recurring problems despite fixes, systemic diagnosis.
- **Feedback Loops:** Reinforcing (amplifying) and balancing (stabilising). Most managers only see reinforcing loops. Apply when: results aren't matching inputs, unintended consequences.
- **System Archetypes:** Fixes that fail, shifting the burden, tragedy of the commons, success to the successful. Apply when: pattern recognition in org behaviour.

**Kyle Norton** — Revenue OS, scaling GTM, talent density as force multiplier.
- **Revenue Operating System:** Process, people, technology, data as four integrated pillars. Fix the weakest pillar first. Apply when: GTM scaling challenges.
- **Six Pillars of Sales Leadership:** Recruit, onboard, develop, retain, promote, exit. In that sequence. Apply when: team building or management challenges.
- **Time Horizon Model:** 0-3 months (execution), 3-12 months (building), 12-36 months (transforming). CRO job evolves across horizons. Apply when: strategic planning.
- **Data Quality as Force Multiplier:** Bad data = bad decisions. Fix data before fixing process. Apply when: CRM trust issues, pipeline accuracy.

## Leadership & Coaching

**Mark Roberge** — Hire for data-predictive traits. Coach to skill gaps. Fix the system, not the person.
- **Sales Hiring Formula:** 5 traits predict success: coachability, curiosity, prior success, intelligence, work ethic. Score all candidates. Apply when: hiring decisions.
- **Skill/Will/System Diagnostic:** Underperformance = one of three root causes. Skill = training. Will = alignment or hard conversation. System = process change. Apply when: rep underperformance diagnosis.
- **Data-Driven Coaching:** Diagnose from data, prescribe specific skill development, measure improvement. Apply when: coaching conversations, rep development.

**Bill Campbell** — Trust first. People first. Everything else follows.
- **Coaching Operating Model:** Only coach the coachable. Trust is the foundation. Radical candor with warmth. Apply when: leadership development, difficult conversations.
- **Team First:** Team > individual. If someone isn't right for the team, move fast. Apply when: team dynamics issues.
- **People Decisions:** Hire for character and fit. Fire quickly when wrong. The cost of delay is enormous. Apply when: personnel decisions.

**Simon Sinek** — Start with Why. Build a Circle of Safety. Play the infinite game.
- **Golden Circle (Start with Why):** Why → How → What. Most companies communicate outside-in. Leaders communicate inside-out. Apply when: vision articulation, team alignment, messaging.
- **Infinite Game:** Finite players play to win. Infinite players play to keep playing. Apply when: long-term strategy vs short-term pressure.
- **Circle of Safety:** Leaders create environments where people feel safe to be vulnerable. Trust before performance. Apply when: team trust issues, culture building.

**Steven Bartlett** — First-principles thinking. Narrative over facts. Context is everything.
- **First-Principles Decomposition:** Break down to fundamental truths. Rebuild from there. Ignore industry convention. Apply when: stuck in conventional thinking.
- **Narrative Construction:** Facts don't change minds. Stories do. Build the narrative, then attach the data. Apply when: exec communication, pitch design.
- **Law of Context:** Same product, different context = different outcome. Apply when: positioning, competitive framing.

## Communication & Structured Thinking

**Barbara Minto** — Structure before substance. The answer goes first.
- **Pyramid Principle:** Start with the answer. Group supporting arguments. MECE at every level. Apply when: any executive communication, email, presentation, memo.
- **SCR Framework:** Situation → Complication → Resolution. Apply when: framing a problem for leadership.
- **MECE (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive):** Categories must not overlap and must cover everything. Apply when: analysis, segmentation, option structuring.
- **So-What Test:** Every point must pass "so what?" If you can't answer it, cut it. Apply when: reviewing any written output for concision.

## Applied Psychology (Contrarian Pair)

**The Cognitive Architect** — Decisions are constructed by the environment, not by reason.
- **System 1/System 2 (Kahneman):** ~95% of decisions are fast/intuitive. If your proposal requires slow deliberation, you've lost. Apply when: proposal design, messaging.
- **Loss Aversion + Prospect Theory:** Losses feel 2x gains. Frame inaction as loss. Apply when: renewals, status quo challenge, churn saves.
- **Pre-Suasion (Cialdini):** The moment BEFORE the message matters more than the message itself. Apply when: discovery openings, subject lines, meeting framing.
- **REDUCE (Berger):** Five barriers to change — Reactance, Endowment, Distance, Uncertainty, Corroborating Evidence. Reduce friction, don't push harder. Apply when: stalled deals, displacement.
- **Choice Architecture + Decoy + Anchoring:** No neutral presentation. Defaults nudge, decoys shift preference, first number = reference point. Apply when: pricing, proposals, competitive positioning.
- **Social vs Market Norms (Ariely):** Introducing market norms into a social relationship is irreversible. Apply when: champion management, free pilots.

**The Depth Psychologist** — Every rational objection has an emotional driver. The emotional driver has an identity at stake.
- **The Shadow (Jung):** Buyer behaviour contradicting stated reasoning = shadow active. Apply when: buyer says one thing, does another.
- **Defence Mechanisms (Freud/Adler):** Rationalisation, projection, displacement, intellectualisation. Name the feeling, don't attack the defence. Apply when: objection doesn't match behaviour.
- **SPICE + Mixing Desk (Dutton):** Five persuasion ingredients (Simplicity, Self-interest, Incongruity, Confidence, Empathy). Psychopathic traits as adjustable dials per context. Apply when: high-stakes communication, competitive bake-offs.
- **Inferiority-Superiority (Adler):** All behaviour compensates for perceived inadequacy. Honour the defence, don't fight it. Apply when: difficult stakeholders, status-conscious buyers.
- **Will to Meaning (Frankl):** Deepest driver is meaning, not pleasure or power. Apply when: champion cultivation, executive sponsor identification.
- **48 Laws + Binary Trap (Greene/Dutton):** Win through actions. Say less. Discover thumbscrews. Introduce third options to break binary framing. Apply when: politics, power dynamics, yes/no deals.

## Financial Intelligence & Deal Economics

**The Financial Architect** — Every company tells a financial story. The commercial opportunity is in the gap between the story and the numbers.
- **Narrative-to-Numbers (Damodaran):** Tell the company's story first, then test each element with a number. Where narrative and numbers diverge = opportunity or risk. Apply when: deal sizing, customer analysis, reading filings.
- **Affordability Corridor (Ramanujam/Damodaran):** Cost-to-serve = floor. Customer economic value = ceiling. Price at 20-35% of ceiling. Express as % of value, never absolute. Apply when: any deal above $100K, LOI design, commercial structuring.
- **Four Failures of Monetisation (Ramanujam):** Feature Shock (overloaded), Minivation (underpriced), Hidden Gem (buried), Undead (should be killed). Apply when: deal structuring, bundle design, pricing review.
- **SaaS Unit Economics Diagnostic (Lemkin/Poyar):** NRR, Gross Margin, CAC Payback, LTV:CAC, Rule of 40, Burn Multiple. Stage-appropriate benchmarks. Apply when: customer health assessment, affordability modelling, benchmarking.
- **Forensic Filing Analysis (Boyle):** Four layers — Headline Story, Structural Story, Cash Story, Unwritten Story. Red flags in each. Apply when: reading prospect filings, pre-deal intelligence, Challenger insight construction.
- **Pricing Waterfall (Ramanujam/Poyar):** List → volume discount → negotiated discount → payment terms → included services → concessions → realised price. 6.6% revenue leakage from unstructured discounting. Apply when: deal approvals, discount governance, margin protection.
- **Value Metric Design (Poyar):** The unit matters more than the price. Three tests: scales with value, easy to measure, predictable. Per-seat vs per-credit vs per-outcome vs hybrid. Apply when: pricing model design, consumption transitions, seat-to-usage migration.
- **Vendor-Side Deal P&L (Practitioner):** TCV minus platform costs, deployment costs, foregone revenue, sales cost, CS cost = net margin. FDE location changes margin by 10-15 points. Apply when: any deal with services component, FDE modelling, internal deal approval.

## GTM Data Intelligence (Added June 2026)

**The GTM Data Strategist** — Your CRM, pipeline, forecast, and AI are only as trustworthy as the data underneath them. Diagnoses data quality before strategy is built on it.
- **Six Dimensions of GTM Data Quality (DAMA):** Completeness, Accuracy, Consistency, Freshness, Validity, Uniqueness. Applied to CRM objects (Account, Contact, Opportunity). Score each dimension independently. The lowest-scoring dimension is the constraint — fix it first. Apply when: any data quality concern, CRM trust issues, pre-analysis validation.
- **Data Science Hierarchy of Needs (Rogati):** Six layers — Collection → Storage → Transformation → Aggregation → Optimisation → AI/ML. Each layer depends on the one below. "Don't jump to AI without building the base." Apply when: AI deployment readiness, lead scoring design, any request that assumes data infrastructure is sound.
- **Expectations-as-Tests (Great Expectations):** Data quality expressed as testable assertions, not opinions. "I expect every Stage 3+ opp to have a non-zero ACV." Pass/fail rates = measurable data quality score. Apply when: pipeline audits, CRM hygiene, data governance design, pre-council-session validation.
- **Signal Separation (Adoption vs Automation):** Product usage data separated into four signal types — Human Adoption (genuine), Automated Batch (1-3 users generating 80%+ activity), Passive Consumption (connected but not used), Phantom Activity (system-generated). Apply when: any renewal analysis involving usage data, adoption scoring, seat utilisation assessment.
- **Pipeline Data Forensics (Five Tests):** Zombie Detection (no activity >2× cycle length), Stage Integrity (stage vs actual qualification), Close Date Accuracy (push count >3 = not real), Duplicate Opportunity Detection (same conversation, multiple opps), Activity Recency (closing in 90 days, no activity in 14). Apply when: pipeline reviews, forecast accuracy diagnosis, zombie elimination.
- **Four C's of Master Data (Taylor):** Code (unique ID), Company (parent-child hierarchy), Category (classification consistency), Country (geographic accuracy). Apply when: territory assignment validation, whitespace analysis, account hierarchy audit.
- **Three-Layer GTM Data Architecture:** 1st Party (CRM, product usage, website — you own), 2nd Party (enrichment vendors (e.g. ZoomInfo, Clay, Clearbit) — vendor provides), 3rd Party (intent, technographic — external signals). Each layer has different quality characteristics and failure modes. Apply when: data quality root cause diagnosis, enrichment conflict resolution, golden record design.
- **Enrichment Health Assessment:** Six metrics — Match Rate (>85%), Fill Rate (>75%), Freshness (<6 months), Decay Rate (<15%), Conflict Rate (<10%), Bounce Rate (<3%). Contact data decays ~30%/year. 14-month-old enrichment is ~35% stale. Apply when: enrichment vendor evaluation, outbound readiness, deliverability diagnosis.

## Marketing Strategy & CMO Operating System (Added June 2026)

**The Marketing Strategist** — Marketing is a system, not a collection of campaigns. The system starts with your marketing advantages and builds everything around accelerating them.
- **Marketing Advantages Framework (Kramer):** 9 advantages in 3 categories — Product (network effects, free plan, wedge), Market/Ecosystem (integrations, channel partnerships, forcing functions), Brand/Story (community/education, founder story, category creation). Score each by strength, competitive differentiation, and combination potential. Advantages drive channel selection and budget, not the reverse. Apply when: marketing strategy design, budget allocation, channel selection, competitive positioning.
- **Seven Strategy Exercises (Kramer):** Sequential exercises building a living marketing operating system: Company Overview → ICP Prioritisation → Marketing Advantages → Perceptions → Positioning → Revenue Levers → Big Bet Campaigns. Each exercise builds on the last. Apply when: annual planning, marketing strategy design, new CMO onboarding, strategy refresh.
- **MOVE Framework (Vajre):** Four phases of revenue growth — Market (find PMF), Operate (build the engine), Velocity (scale what works), Expand (grow within accounts). Each phase requires different marketing motions. Most common mistake: skipping Operate (systematise) and jumping to Velocity (scale). Apply when: diagnosing marketing-business misalignment, setting expectations, phase-appropriate activity selection.
- **FlipMyFunnel / ABM (Vajre):** Invert the traditional funnel: Identify → Expand → Engage → Advocate → Measure. Start with target accounts, map buying committees, engage with relevant content, turn into advocates. Three tiers: 1:1 (top 10-20), 1:Few (50-200), 1:Many (200-1000+). Apply when: ABM strategy design, marketing-sales alignment, account-based campaign architecture.
- **Four Revenue Levers (Kramer):** Stack-rank the four ways marketing drives revenue: (1) Grow ToFu with core audience, (2) Grow ToFu with new audience, (3) Increase customer value, (4) Increase efficiency. Focus on top 1-2. Apply when: budget allocation, marketing prioritisation, cross-functional alignment on marketing's role.
- **Perceptions Framework (Kramer):** 3-5 key narratives written from the audience's POV as beliefs they'd repeat back. Company insight, market insight, product insight. Status: Established/Emerging/New. All content ladders to perceptions. Apply when: content strategy, messaging architecture, campaign review, brand building.
- **Fuel/Engine/Audience Model (Kramer):** Every marketing motion has three components: Fuel (story/content), Engine (distribution/channels), Audience (ICP segment). Define in order: Audience → Fuel → Engine. Apply when: campaign design, channel selection, diagnosing why campaigns aren't working.
- **ABM Maturity Model (Vajre):** Five stages from Traditional (lead-based) → Aware → Aligned → Orchestrated → Predictive. Infrastructure must match maturity stage. Buying an ABM platform at Stage 0-1 = expensive shelfware. Apply when: ABM programme design, ABM tool evaluation, marketing-sales alignment assessment.

## M&A & Corporate Development (Added June 2026)

### F-116: Acquisition Evaluation Framework
**Avatars:** Financial Architect (primary — filing analysis, deal economics), Category Architect (M&A as growth engine), Marketing Strategist (marketing advantage assessment)
**Source:** [TD — Damodaran, Category Architect, practitioner methodology]
**When to invoke:** Evaluating acquisition targets, partnership opportunities, "should we buy this company?", competitive acquisition analysis, post-acquisition integration planning
**Core:** Every acquisition must answer five questions before financial modelling begins:
1. **Strategic rationale:** Classify as primary + secondary + conditional. Primary types: Capability acquisition (buying what you can't build — compounds), Revenue acquisition (buying customers — decays without integration), Talent acquisition / Acquihire (buying people — leaks within 18 months), Brand acquisition (buying market position — valuable in niche/regional markets). Most acquisitions are composites, not single-type. Name the primary driver and any secondary/conditional layers.
2. **Capability gap test:** What does the target do that we cannot do in 12 months of internal development? If you can build it in 12 months, the acquisition premium is unjustifiable.
3. **Integration complexity:** How many systems, processes, and teams must merge? Score 1-5. Above 3 = integration will consume more resources than the acquisition saves. "Most acquisitions fail not because the target was wrong but because the integration was underestimated" (Category Architect).
4. **Customer overlap:** What percentage of the target's customers are already your customers? High overlap = revenue synergy overstated. Low overlap = new market access but unproven fit.
5. **Kill criteria:** Under what conditions would you walk away? Define before negotiations begin. If you can't articulate kill criteria, you'll negotiate emotionally.
**Financial layer (after strategic pass):** Apply Financial Architect's Narrative-to-Numbers (tell the target's story, test with numbers), Forensic Filing Analysis (4 layers), and Affordability Corridor (floor = integration cost + acquisition premium; ceiling = strategic value of the capability gap closed). For M&A specifically: apply standard addbacks (salaries, director payouts, one-time costs) to EBITDA before calculating multiples. The acquiring company absorbs people into its cost structure — raw EBITDA understates the earning power of the acquired asset.

## Diagnostic & Risk Assessment (Added May 2026)

### F-081: Baseline x Deviation Risk Model
**Avatars:** Meadows (systems), McMahon (qualification), Depth Psychologist (behaviour)
**When to invoke:** Any account risk scoring, renewal analysis, churn prediction
**Core:** Risk = Signal Strength x Baseline Weakness. Calls/emails are baseline (ground truth). Market signals create deviation. False positives come from signals. Strong baselines absorb signals. Weak baselines amplify them.
**Counter-intuition:** Same signals, opposite outcomes depending on baseline strength.

### F-082: Over-Correction / Confidence Bias Window
**Avatars:** Cognitive Architect (bias analysis), Van der Kooij (revenue architecture), Minto (logical structure)
**When to invoke:** Competitive displacement analysis, consumption pricing strategy, market positioning
**Core:** Companies over-correct toward cheaper tools driven by assumed confidence bias. They haven't validated the gap. When data quality degrades at scale (6-12 months), they return — but to consumption, not seats.
**MECE split required:** Over-correctors (irrational, will return) vs Rational consolidators (structural gap, won't return). Different intervention for each.
**Inversion test (Cognitive Architect):** "What if the correction is permanent?" Must be answered before committing resources.

### F-083: Defensive Wall vs Offensive Play
**Avatars:** McMahon (deal management), Reimer (strategic accounts), Depth Psychologist (behaviour)
**When to invoke:** Deciding WHO should intervene on at-risk accounts and HOW
**Core:** Talk tracks = defensive wall (prevents MEDIUM to HIGH). Exec intervention = offensive play (saves accounts past the wall). Never conflate. Don't send AM to fix HIGHEST. Don't deploy the operator on MEDIUM.

### F-084: Loop Transition Detection
**Avatars:** Meadows (feedback loops), Van der Kooij (rate of change)
**When to invoke:** Monday forecast, quarterly reviews, engagement trend analysis
**Core:** Accounts exist in either virtuous loops (engagement to value to engagement) or vicious loops (disengagement to doubt to alternatives to disengagement). The leverage point is at the TRANSITION — when the loop switches polarity.
**Detection:** "Is engagement INCREASING or DECREASING vs last quarter?" Decreasing + signals = loop transition in progress.

### F-085: Permissible Neglect Pattern
**Avatars:** Depth Psychologist (shadow), Greene (power dynamics), Adler (inferiority/compensation)
**When to invoke:** When AM performance issues appear systemic rather than individual
**Core:** When high-performing AMs publicly acknowledge abandoning six-figure accounts without consequence, the organisation has an implicit permission structure around neglect. Root cause is capacity constraint (7 AMs / $9M+), not laziness. Fix the system, not just the behaviour.

### F-086: Strategic Value Creation (Reimer Extension)
**Avatars:** Reimer (mega deal), Sinek (purpose), Drucker (contribution)
**When to invoke:** Accounts at HIGHEST risk where AM credibility is already lost
**Core:** Don't respond to their RFP as a vendor. CREATE a strategic initiative that solves their unsolved business problem (not their vendor problem). Offer as time-boxed proof of partnership at no cost. Changes frame from "which vendor is cheapest" to "which partner can fix our data."

### F-087: Council Interrogation Filter (Three Tests)
**Avatars:** All (meta-framework applied TO council output)
**When to invoke:** After any council session before converting opinions to action
**Core:** Filter every council opinion through Three Tests:
1. Is this true? (What evidence from our data?)
2. Does this matter? (What's the magnitude?)
3. Is this actionable? (What changes THIS WEEK?)
Signal = survives all three. Changes behaviour.
Noise = true but not actionable. Changes thinking without changing behaviour.

### F-090: Operating Cadence Architecture (5P Standard)
**Avatars:** Revenue Architect (process discipline), Norton (Revenue OS), Drucker (effectiveness)
**Source:** [NEON/operating-cadence-designer]
**When to invoke:** "Our meetings are status updates," "nobody makes decisions," redesigning meeting architecture, installing decision cadence

### F-104: Change Impact Classification (Traffic Light)
**Avatars:** Norton (source — Kyle Norton framework), Campbell (people-side change)
**Source:** [NEON/revops-change-management — Kyle Norton, CRO Owner.com]
**When to invoke:** Before communicating any GTM change, territory redesign, comp plan change, tool migration, methodology rollout
**Core:** Classify every change by impact BEFORE designing communication: Green (low — written update sufficient), Yellow (medium — written + video), Red (high — minimum three touchpoints: written, video, AND live training). Most orgs default to Green for everything. That's why adoption fails.
**Red change communication sequence:** Context (2-4 weeks before, WHY before WHAT) → Vision (1-2 weeks, reveal change + connect to WHY) → Detail (at launch, everything needed to operate) → Feedback (weeks 1-4, dedicated channels) → Reinforcement (months 1-3, celebrate wins, correct drift).

### F-105: Reinforcement Architecture (Forgetting Curve + Reverse Salient)
**Avatars:** Norton (source — Kyle Norton frameworks), Campbell (trust)
**Source:** [NEON/revops-change-management — Kyle Norton, Ebbinghaus]
**When to invoke:** Enablement programme design, coaching methodology, "we trained them but nothing changed," post-change adoption
**Core principles:**
- **One Variable at a Time:** Only change one thing at a time. Run it with depth over breadth. A rollout is a programme, not an announcement. If changing multiple things simultaneously, that's the constraint — stop, pick one.
- **Forgetting Curve:** Without reinforcement: 50% forgotten within 24 hours, 80% within one month. Reinforcement cadence: same day → next day → 2 days → 1 week → increasing intervals. Goal: unconscious competence.
- **Reverse Salient:** Before coaching anything, find the single biggest bottleneck. Sales is sequential — no point coaching objection handling if attention isn't earned in the first 5 seconds. Start with data. Pick one area. Go deep.
- **Bloom's Taxonomy for Enablement:** Remember → Understand → Apply → Analyse → Evaluate → Create. Most training stays at Remember. Standard is Create — reps build their own tools. Every programme must include a creation task.
- **Organisational Energy:** Adoption doesn't coast on willpower. Design the energy system: visible scoreboards, daily standups referencing the one thing, dedicated channel, manager 1:1s structured around it, public recognition.

### F-111: Three-Tier KPI Architecture
**Avatars:** Norton (delivery fit — Revenue OS context), Meadows (systems), Minto (structure)

### F-112: SPICED Scoring Rubric + Stage-Gating

**Stage-gating (minimum SPICED scores):**

**Core:** Eight roles: Economic Buyer (budget authority, final yes/no), Champion (advocates + credibility + personal win), User Buyer (daily user, workflow impact), Technical Buyer (integration/security/compliance), Coach/Mentor (trusted EB advisor, no formal authority), Initiator (raised the need, often NOT the EB), Legal/Procurement (paper process), Saboteur (status quo incentive, budget competitor).
**Critical classification rule:** Champions require EXPLICIT engagement evidence (CRM activity, demo attended, prior emails). Title alone is never sufficient. Without evidence, classify as Influencer > Potential Champion. Most "Champions" AMs report are actually Coaches or Initiators. Test: can they sell when you're not in the room? Will they give you access to the EB?
**Connects to McMahon:** Extends MEDDPICC's Champion and Economic Buyer concepts with operational classification rigour. McMahon identifies the roles; this framework prevents mis-classification.

### F-114: Four-Axis Account Scoring
**Avatars:** Norton (delivery fit — Revenue OS context), Revenue Architect (CRO — territory prioritisation)
**Source:** [Practitioner � account scoring]
**When to invoke:** Account prioritisation, territory planning, ABM list building, "which accounts should I work first?"
**Core:** Score each account on four independent axes (each 0-100), then compute weighted composite:
| Axis | Question | Default Weight | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fit | Does this match our ICP? | 45% | `enrich_companies` vs ICP criteria |
| Intent | Are they actively researching? | 25% | `enrich_intent` (unconstrained topics) |
| Trigger | Fresh event creating a window? | 25% | `enrich_news` + `enrich_scoops` (last 90d) |
| Engagement | Already interacting with us? | 5% | `account_research` narrative |
**Tiering:** A (≥75 — route to AE for 1:1 outreach within 24h), B (50-74 — SDR sequence, ABM retargeting), C (<50 — nurture, monitor for signal change).
**Key principles:** Composite is always a transparent weighted sum — never opaque. Every score must be explainable. "Why now" per account cites a SPECIFIC signal, not the composite restated. Weights are exposed and overridable by user.

### F-115: Relationship Posture Classification
**Avatars:** Voss (tactical empathy — reading the room), Reimer (strategic accounts — relationship depth)
**Source:** [Practitioner � meeting preparation]
**When to invoke:** Meeting preparation, account strategy, stakeholder mapping, re-engagement planning
**Core:** Classify each contact's relationship posture before designing approach:
| Posture | Definition | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Cold | No prior interaction signal | Standard outreach required |
| Warm-but-dormant | Significant past engagement but no current activity | **Highest leverage** — relationship exists, needs reactivation |
| Active | Current engagement (recent calls, emails, meetings) | Maintain and deepen |
| Hostile | Negative signal (rejected, complained, competitor advocate) | Handle with care or route around |
**Key insight:** Warm-but-dormant is the most valuable and most missed classification. Requires cross-referencing historical engagement across companies and roles. A VP who was your champion at their previous company and just joined a target account is Warm-but-dormant — not Cold.
**Connects to Voss:** Tactical Empathy calibrates HOW to approach each posture. Cold = curiosity-led. Warm-but-dormant = reference past shared context. Active = deepen through labelling. Hostile = acknowledge and defuse.
