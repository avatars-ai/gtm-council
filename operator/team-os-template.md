## 1. Operating Philosophy (Personal)

- **Gut then data.** Trust instinct first, validate with evidence. Intuition-led, evidence-confirmed.
- **Sparring partner, not assistant.** Challenge assumptions, find blind spots, create "how did you think of that?" moments. Never just validate.
- Sales requires innate pattern recognition and judgement — not everyone can be taught to be a great salesperson.
- Challenge customer assumptions. Ensure fact/logic-based decisions. Consultative truth-telling over appeasement. Avoid short-term thinking at all costs.
- Rejects: "sales = pitching", "more pipeline = more sales", "everyone can be taught."

## 2. Coaching & Leadership

### Philosophy
- **Discipline > motivation.** Be boring, be consistent, be repeatable.
- **Self-development is non-negotiable.** Won't chase anyone to develop themselves. Ownership is the individual's. Waiting to be told what to learn = already fallen behind.
- **Radical candor with empathy.** Direct truth-telling wrapped in care. Rips the band-aid off.
- **Focus on strengths.** "Can't teach fish to climb tree." Data-driven talent optimisation. Show historical data of where they're prolific vs struggling.

### 1:1 Structure
1. Rep drives agenda (not manager)
2. Facts/figures prepped in advance (<5 min review)
3. Forecasting completed quickly (first 10 min)
4. Deep dive on ONE topic — go deep, not wide
5. Apply learnings to 2-3 other situations

### Tiered Management
- **Strategic tier** (~40% coaching time): Large accounts $250K+. Operator personally involved. Weekly deal reviews.
- **Enterprise tier** (~30%): Self-sufficient reps. Exception-based management.
- **Mid-Market tier** (~30%): Activity and process management. Structured operating rhythm.

### AM Evaluation Criteria
Raw performance data, frameworks applied to deal structure, whole book management (not account marking), communication style, self-development interests.

### Signs AM Is Struggling
Fluffy answers, blaming others, over-optimism that falls apart on inspection. Look for substance vs performance in communication.

## 3. Commercial & Deal Strategy

### Deal Principles
- **Chess-board thinking (IFTT):** If they say X → respond Y. If competitor does A → counter B. Map 2-3 moves ahead.
- **Bezos Type 1/Type 2 (applied):** Type 1 = mega deal, final churn, 3yr contract. Type 2 = early upsell, prelim pricing. Most AMs treat everything as Type 1.
- **Three-data-point rule** applies to deal patterns across the book.
- Reframe "upsell" to "restructure" when engagement exceeds contract structure.

### MEDDPICC (Applied)
- **Metrics:** Quantified business impact, not vague "save time"
- **Economic Buyer:** Can they sign the PO? (Not "do they want to?")
- **Champion test:** Will they sell internally when you're not in the room? If not, they're a coach.
- **Paper Process (McMahon):** At 120 days to close, map who signs, what order, what docs, what timeline. Can't map it = at risk.
- **Competition:** Named competitors + "do nothing" + "build it ourselves"

### SPICED Discovery (Van der Kooij)
Situation → Pain → Impact → Critical Event → Decision. Sequential WITH MEDDPICC — SPICED informs the conversation, MEDDPICC qualifies the deal.

### Negotiation
- **Diamond prep (>$75K):** Full 20-step system. Core: emotions/perceptions > power/logic.
- **Abbreviated (standard renewals):** 7-question version.
- **Karrass:** Diminishing concessions. Never concede without getting. Never first major concession. Patience = advantage.
- **Voss 3 Yes types:** Commitment (real), Confirmation (fact), Counterfeit (escape). Test with: "What specifically happens next?"
- **Malhotra contingent contracts:** When parties disagree about the future, structure deal for both scenarios.
- Pro-rated full access beats free extension (confidence signal, skin in the game).

### Churn Save Protocol
Tri-pronged simultaneous outreach: CEO 8am (operator), Budget Owner 8:15am (operator), Ops Champions 9am (AM). Same day. Never single-threaded.

### Winback Seed
Thank team by name. Leave standalone offers active. State specific follow-up month. Signal triggers: hiring, competitor renewal, product launches, person movement. Never burn the bridge.

## 5. Operational Standards

### Forecast Categories
- **Commit ([X]%):** Champion confirmed, EB engaged, paper process mapped
- **Best Case ([X]%):** High probability, one variable unresolved
- **Pipeline ([X-Y]%):** Qualified, progressing, multiple variables remain
- **Upside ([X-Y]%):** Positive signals, not yet qualified
- Never forecast on words. Forecast on actions (EB engaged? Procurement started? Timeline shared?).
- Exception-based reporting: walk through what changed, what's at risk, what's new.

### Pipeline Standards
- **Coverage:** Strategic [X-Y]× | Enterprise [X-Y]× | Mid-Market [X-Y]×
- **Stage integrity:** No Stage 3 without confirmed champion. No Stage 4 without engaged EB. No Stage 5 without mapped paper process.
- **Hygiene rhythm:** Weekly (>$50K next-step review), Bi-weekly (ghost opps — kill or re-engage), Monthly (full scrub), Quarterly (territory rebalance)
- Any deal past expected close without documented reason → review within 48 hours.

### Risk Scoring: Baseline × Deviation (May 2026)

Risk = Signal Strength × Baseline Weakness. Calls/emails are baseline (ground truth). Signals are deviation forces. Strong baselines absorb signals. Weak baselines amplify them.

**Baseline (from Chorus + Email):**
- STRONG: monthly+ calls, multi-threaded, customer bringing problems
- MODERATE: quarterly calls, single-threaded, proceeding normally
- WEAK: calls declining, ghosting, competitive eval in progress
- UNKNOWN: no Chorus in 12+ months — treat as WEAK

**6 Churn Signals:**
- (A) Over-Correction/Cheaper Alternative
- (B) Product-Market Doubt
- (C) Build vs Buy/AI Confidence
- (D) Usage/Adoption Failure
- (E) Org Restructuring/Inherited Risk
- (F) Vendor Consolidation Mandate

**Matrix:**
- Strong + 0-2 signals = LOW
- Moderate + 2 signals = MEDIUM
- Weak + 2 signals = HIGH
- Weak/Unknown + 3+ signals = HIGHEST

### GARR Gate (Mandatory before retention analysis)

**Formula:** GARR = (Renewal Value + Upsells Won + LTC) / (Expiring Contracts + LTC)
- LTC = multi-year contracts where ANNIVERSARY DATE falls in window (not all active contracts)
- Expiring = ALL contracts with expiration dates in window (renewals + co-termed upsells)
- AM-level: use your CRM or BI exports. Calibrate to your authoritative team GARR baseline.
- **Policy:** [Configure your co-term and minimum contract policies here.]

### Churn Categories (Sonar Model)
- **Deterministic:** External force beyond AM control (RIF, termination, build-vs-buy). Talk track irrelevant.
- **Addressable:** Saveable at 40-60% with better intervention.
- **Should-Not-Have-Churned:** Standard AM execution prevents entirely.
- **MECE split:** Over-corrector (price-driven, users still prefer your product, will return to consumption) vs Rational consolidator (genuine product gap, won't return). Different intervention for each.

### 5 Execution Gates (Team Standards)

1. **90-Day Pre-Renewal MEDDPICC Gate:** No renewal enters Stage 3 without: champion confirmed, EB engaged, paper process mapped, competitive landscape documented. The operator reviews every renewal at 90-day mark in [Your Forecast Cadence].
2. **Permissible Neglect Standard:** Every account >$25K ACV must have 1 substantive call per quarter in Chorus. System must catch what AMs deprioritise.
3. **MECE Classification Protocol:** Every MEDIUM+ risk H2 account classified as over-corrector or rational consolidator before intervention designed. Classification requires evidence.
4. **Strategic Value Creation for HIGHEST Risk:** Don't respond to RFP. Create a strategic initiative solving the customer's business problem. The operator owns these calls personally.
5. **Monday Forecast Engagement Question:** "Is our engagement frequency INCREASING or DECREASING versus last quarter?" — for every renewal in 120-day window. Evidence-based answers only.

### Talk Track Function
Talk tracks = defensive wall (prevents MEDIUM from becoming HIGH/HIGHEST). Exec intervention = offensive play (saves accounts past the wall). Never conflate. Don't send AM to fix HIGHEST. Don't deploy the operator on MEDIUM.

### Book Triage Protocol (When AMs Leave)
1. [Region A] HQ accounts: push to [Region A] teams
2. [Remaining] accounts: redistribute by geography/tier/capacity to existing team
3. New A-player book: $50K+ ACV, 90+ day runway, mix protect/grow, 15-20 accounts, ~$1.5M
