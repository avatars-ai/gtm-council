# Protocol: GTM Strategy

## Scope

This protocol governs council sessions focused on end-to-end go-to-market strategy,
including but not limited to:

- Market positioning and category creation/defence
- Pricing and packaging (subscription, consumption, hybrid)
- Org design (team structure, roles, territories, comp)
- Channel strategy (direct, partnerships, OEM, agency)
- International expansion and localisation
- Revenue architecture (NRR, pipeline, forecasting models)
- Competitive strategy at the market level
- Product-market fit assessment and evolution

## Pre-Session Data Gathering

**This section supplements the Mandatory Data-Gathering Protocol in SKILL.md.**
The SKILL.md protocol (Steps 1-5) runs FIRST. GTM strategy questions are typically
**Scope B (Territory/Book)** — use aggregate queries with targeted deep-dives,
NOT per-account full sweeps across the entire book.

Before any GTM strategy session, gather:

### From Tools
- **Salesforce:** Pipeline summary, win/loss trends, segment performance, territory data
- **the platform:** Market intelligence, competitor signals, intent data trends, TAM analysis
- **Chorus:** Aggregate call themes, objection patterns, competitive mentions

### From User
- Current state description
- Specific strategic question or decision
- Constraints (budget, headcount, timeline, political)
- What's already been tried or considered

## Avatar Engagement Rules for GTM Strategy

### Required Perspectives
Every GTM strategy session must include at least:
1. **A strategist** — Someone who thinks about market positioning and competitive dynamics
2. **An operator** — Someone who thinks about execution, systems, and scalability
3. **A challenger** — Someone who will invert the question and find the failure modes

### Framing Questions Every Avatar Must Address
1. **Where to play?** — Which segments, geographies, personas, use cases
2. **How to win?** — What's the defensible advantage in those segments
3. **What capabilities are required?** — People, process, technology, data
4. **What's the sequence?** — What comes first, second, third — and why that order
5. **What kills this?** — Inversion: how does this strategy fail?

## Output Requirements

### Strategy sessions must include:
- **Current state assessment** — Where are we now (evidence-based)
- **Strategic options** — At least 2 distinct paths (not just "do this")
- **Trade-offs** — What you gain and lose with each option
- **Sequencing** — What to do first, and what depends on what
- **Kill criteria** — How do we know if this isn't working (leading indicators)
- **Resource requirements** — What this costs in people, money, time, attention

### Operator-Specific Context
- Adapt strategy depth and framing to the operator's level and scope
- Check `operator/team-os.md` (if customised) for the operator's strategic frameworks,
  current initiatives, and growth targets
- Default to systemic thinking, MECE frameworks, and evidence over opinion
- Apply Roger Martin's Strategy Choice Cascade for strategic options
- Apply Collins' "fire bullets then cannonballs" — test small, scale what works
- Apply Munger's inversion — "how does this fail?" is always a valid question
