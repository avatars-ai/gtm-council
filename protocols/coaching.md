# Protocol: Coaching

*Supplements the Mandatory Data-Gathering Protocol in SKILL.md (Steps 1-5).
Coaching questions are Scope C — rep's Salesforce data + rep's Chorus calls.*

## Step 0: Call-Type Classification (Mandatory — Run Before Any Coaching)

Every coaching session starts by classifying the call. The standard applied must match the call type. Applying renewal-level scrutiny to a first discovery call is a calibration error — it destroys rep confidence without improving outcomes.

| Call Type | Relationship Stage | Primary Success Metric |
|---|---|---|
| First discovery / intro call | Net-new — no prior relationship | Did they uncover a real problem? Are next steps committed? |
| Second call / follow-up | Early engagement | Did the rep move from rapport to commercial? Is a hypothesis being tested? |
| Technical deep-dive | Mid-process | Did the rep frame the business problem and enable the expert — or go passive? |
| Executive alignment / intro to power | Existing customer | Credibility established in first 90 seconds? Did not pitch before diagnosing? |
| C-suite strategy call | Existing customer | Business impact framing throughout. Clear ask, clean exit. No product features. |
| Manager joining rep's call | Any | Did the senior person accelerate the deal or take it over? Was rep ownership preserved? |
| Expansion / upsell call | Existing customer | Current usage and ROI referenced. Specific gap identified. Growth conversation, not re-sale. |
| Renewal discovery call | Existing customer — contract expiring | Usage data pulled. Churn signals surfaced. No downgrade discussed before expansion explored. |
| Churn save / rescue call | At-risk customer | De-escalation without capitulation. Prorated access offered, not free extension. |
| QBR / business review | Existing customer | Felt strategic, not administrative. Buyer outcomes owned. |
| Pricing / commercial call | Any | Rep anchored — did not open with a concession. Paper process mapped. |
| Stalled deal re-engagement | Ghosted prospect | Created movement without desperation. Specific call-to-action with a deadline. |
| POC / trial kick-off | New or expanding customer | Success criteria co-owned before the POC begins. Scope, timeline, measurable outcome all confirmed. |

**Preparation standard by call type:**
- First discovery: ICP research done. No use of existing customer data (there is none).
- Executive call: Credibility anchor ready. Business impact hypothesis formed. One clear ask prepared.
- Renewal / expansion: Usage data pulled before the call. ACV and expiry confirmed. ROI framing ready.
- Churn save: Full account history reviewed. Tri-pronged stakeholder plan ready. Decision on save offer level made before dialling.
- QBR: Outcomes from last review mapped. Progress evidenced with data, not narrative.

**Context check before escalating feedback:**
Before applying high-intensity coaching, verify: *Is this genuinely poor performance for this call type and relationship stage — or is the wrong standard being applied?*
A first discovery call where a real problem was uncovered and next steps are committed is success, even if advanced commercial techniques were absent.

## Diagnostic Before Coaching

1. **Skill/Will/System (Roberge):** Is this a training gap, motivation gap, or process gap?
2. **Task-Relevant Maturity (Grove):** TRM is task-specific, not person-specific. An expert AM can have low TRM on a new task. Low = directive. Medium = coaching. High = delegative.
3. **Passenger Diagnostic (Slootman):** "Did it really matter you were there?" Pleasant + no outcomes = passenger.
4. **Neediness Check (Camp):** Chasing after silence, unprompted concessions, vague next steps, talking > listening, forecasting on hope.

## Coaching Session Structure

1. Pull rep's pipeline data, win rates, activity, attainment (Salesforce)
2. Pull rep's recent calls — discovery quality, talk time, objection handling (Chorus)
3. Identify patterns — what behaviours repeat across deals?
4. Diagnose root cause (Skill/Will/System)
5. Prescribe specific development with measurable outcomes
6. Deep-dive specific deals only if coaching conversation surfaces a deal-level issue

## Operator Coaching Philosophy

- **Discipline > motivation.** Be boring, be consistent, be repeatable.
- **Self-development is non-negotiable.** Will not chase someone to develop themselves.
- **Radical candor with empathy.** Rip the band-aid off, wrapped in care.
- **Focus on strengths.** "Can't teach fish to climb tree." Data-driven talent optimisation.
- **Signs AM is struggling:** Fluffy answers, blaming others, over-optimism that falls apart on inspection.
- **Ideal 1:1:** AM drives agenda, facts/figures prepped, forecasting done quickly, then deep-dive on topic/account/deal.

## Peacetime vs Wartime (Horowitz)

Coach AMs to switch modes deliberately. Wartime = decisive execution under pressure. Peacetime = explore, build, experiment. Permanent wartime burns teams.
