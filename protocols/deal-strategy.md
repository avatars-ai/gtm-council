# Protocol: Deal Strategy

*Supplements the Mandatory Data-Gathering Protocol in SKILL.md (Steps 1-5).
Deal questions are Scope A — run the full 7-source sweep.*

## Avatar Engagement Rules

Every deal session must include perspectives from:
1. **Deal strategist** — deal architecture and qualification
2. **Negotiation expert** — leverage, concessions, terms
3. **Psychology lens** — buyer motivation, stakeholder dynamics

## Deal Classification

| Deal Type | Approach | Key Frameworks |
|---|---|---|
| Renewal (standard) | McMahon paper process, abbreviated Diamond (7-question) | Paper process at 120 days |
| Renewal (at risk) | Tri-pronged simultaneous outreach (CEO 8am, Budget Owner 8:15am, Ops 9am) | Never single-threaded on a save |
| Expansion | SPICED discovery + consumption data analysis | Value realisation → expansion bridge |
| Competitive displacement | Battle card system, landmine questions | 7-stage spine, 5 moats |
| Mega-deal ($250K+) | Full Diamond 20-step, council session mandatory | Reimer Two Mountain, MEDDPICC deep |
| Winback | Seed protocol, signal monitoring | Bridge-burning prevention, trigger-based |

## Output Requirements

Every deal session must produce:
- **Deal health** — MEDDPICC-based, not gut feel
- **Stakeholder map** — positions and gaps in coverage
- **Competitive position** — where we stand
- **Risk register** — ranked by probability × impact
- **Negotiation stance** — opening, walk-away, concession strategy
- **Next steps** — action, owner, date (no ambiguity)
- **Confidence level** — High/Medium/Low with reasoning

## Operator Deal Principles

- Bezos Type 1/Type 2 for deal decisions (irreversible vs reversible)
- Karrass concession pattern (diminishing, never first major concession)
- Voss 3 types of Yes (Commitment/Confirmation/Counterfeit)
- Three-data-point rule: one = event, two = coincidence, three = pattern
- Pro-rated full access beats free extension (confidence signal)
- Reframe "upsell" to "restructure" when engagement exceeds contract structure

## Lessons: Large Enterprise Engagement

- Exhaust all 7 data sources on first pass — never make user ask "did you check X?"
- For PE-backed accounts with revenue targets, build revenue model proactively
- Evidence provenance mandatory: Directly Evidenced / Externally Sourced / Inferred / Constructed
- Dual-track delivery: top-down (exec access) + bottom-up (evidence) + customer-authored recommendation

## Document Delivery: Pre-Suasion Layer
*(Added June 2026 — Dotdigital POV session)*

The effectiveness of any customer-facing document depends partly on the mental state of the reader at the moment of opening — not just the document's content. Design the delivery mechanism alongside the document.

**The subject line IS pre-suasion.**
Whatever the reader thinks about immediately before opening the document becomes the interpretive lens for everything inside it. A subject line that primes for "vendor proposal" will cause a C-level reader to evaluate even independent analytical content through a commercial suspicion filter.

**Three subject line registers:**

| Register | Format | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| **Analyst frame** | *"What [company]'s [data source] says about [opportunity]"* | Default for cold C-level reader, no prior relationship |
| **Strategic frame** | *"[Data point] — ahead of [upcoming meeting/date]"* | When there is already a meeting to anchor to |
| **Low-pressure (Braun)** | *"A data point I thought worth sharing"* | When minimising resistance is the priority; new relationship |

**Primer call rule.**
A document that arrives after a conversation gets read as a strategic recommendation. A document that arrives cold gets read as a proposal. For any document going to an Economic Buyer the operator has not yet met: brief the Mobiliser first. Let the Mobiliser position the document with the Economic Buyer before it lands in their inbox.

**Mobiliser briefing checklist:**
- Walk through the argument verbally (15 minutes) before Mobiliser sees the document
- Identify which finding is most likely to trigger a defensive response and prepare the Mobiliser to handle it
- Confirm the Mobiliser can present the document as their own strategic thinking — remove any language that only makes sense coming from the vendor
- Agree the subject line framing together
