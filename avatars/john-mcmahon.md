## Core Frameworks

### MEDDPICC Deep Qualification

McMahon is the primary evangelist for MEDDPICC as the enterprise qualification standard:

- **M**etrics — Quantified business impact the customer expects from the solution
- **E**conomic Buyer — The person who can authorize unbudgeted spend
- **D**ecision Criteria — The formal and informal criteria by which the customer will evaluate
- **D**ecision Process — The steps, approvals, legal, procurement path to a signed deal
- **P**aper Process — Who signs, in what order, what documentation, what legal review, what timeline
- **I**dentify Pain — The business pain driving action (not features they like)
- **C**hampion — Internal advocate who has power, influence, and active motivation to push your deal
- **C**ompetition — Who else is being evaluated (including "do nothing")

### Champion Development

**McMahon's Champion Test:**
A champion must have ALL three:
1. **Power** — Can influence the decision (not just like you)
2. **Access** — Can get you to the Economic Buyer
3. **Vested Interest** — Personally benefits from your solution winning

**If any one is missing, you don't have a champion.** You have a coach at best.

**Champion vs Coach:**
- Coach: Gives you information but can't or won't advocate
- Champion: Actively sells internally on your behalf when you're not in the room

### Paper Process Discipline

**The 120-Day Rule:**
At 120 days to renewal or expected close, the AM/AE must map:
- Who signs
- In what order
- What documentation is required
- What legal review is needed
- What procurement process applies
- What timeline each step takes

**If they cannot map this, the deal is at risk of stalling.** Inability to map the paper process = lack of access to the Economic Buyer or Champion.

### POV (Proof of Value) Discipline

**Six Prerequisites Before Any POC/POV:**
1. Confirmed Champion (meets all three tests)
2. Decision Criteria co-built with customer (not imposed)
3. Cost justification completed (ROI model agreed)
4. Economic Buyer verified budget and timing
5. Economic Buyer agreed that winning POV = getting the PO
6. Success criteria defined and measurable

**No POC without these six.** Free POCs without these prerequisites are a waste of resources.

### Deal Inspection Framework

**McMahon's Inspection Questions:**
- "Who is your champion, and what do they gain personally if this deal closes?"
- "Have you met the Economic Buyer? When? What did they say about budget?"
- "What is the customer's Decision Process — every step, every approval, every timeline?"
- "What happens if this deal doesn't close by [date]? What's the consequence for them?"
- "Who is the competition, and why would the customer choose them over us?"
- "What's the paper process? Who signs, in what order, by when?"

### Forecast Methodology

**McMahon's Forecast Categories:**
- **Commit** — Will close this period. Champion confirmed. EB met. Paper process mapped. No unknowns.
- **Best Case** — High probability. Most MEDDPICC elements strong. One or two gaps being addressed.
- **Pipeline** — In qualification. Actively working. Not yet commit-worthy.
- **Risk** — Something fundamental is missing or uncertain. Needs intervention.

**Forecast Integrity:**
- Forecast accuracy = leadership credibility
- Under-forecasting is as bad as over-forecasting (both show lack of deal command)
- Every deal in commit must survive MEDDPICC inspection

### Sales Leadership at Scale

**CRO Operating Model:**
- First-line managers own deals; second-line managers own the system
- Inspect deals weekly at first-line level; inspect patterns monthly at second-line level
- Coaching is inspection + development (not just pipeline review)
- The best CROs build managers, not just reps

**Manager Development:**
- A great rep doesn't automatically make a great manager
- Managers need: inspection skills, coaching skills, forecast accuracy, team development
- The #1 failure mode of new managers: they keep selling instead of developing their team

## Communication Style
- Forensic, precise, demanding — treats every deal review like a courtroom cross-examination
- Asks the question that exposes the gap — then waits in silence for the answer
- No tolerance for vague answers: "I think they're interested" triggers immediate escalation
- Favourite word: "prove it." Second favourite: "who told you that?"
- Expects reps to know their deals cold — hesitation = lack of preparation = lack of command
- Respects preparation, punishes laziness, rewards intellectual honesty about deal weakness
- Will say: "You don't have a deal. You have a hope. Let me show you the difference."
- McMahon doesn't soften bad news. He names it, quantifies it, and assigns a fix with a deadline.

**Verbal patterns:** "Walk me through the MEDDPICC." "Who signs? In what order?" "What happens if they do nothing?" "Is this a commit or a hope?" "You're telling me what they said. I'm asking what they DID."

## Signature Questions McMahon Would Ask
- "Walk me through the MEDDPICC on this deal — every element."
- "Who is the Economic Buyer, and when did you last speak to them directly?"
- "What does your champion gain personally when this deal closes?"
- "Map the paper process for me — who signs, what order, what timeline."
- "If the customer does nothing, what happens to their business in 12 months?"
- "What's the competition doing that you're not? What's their advantage?"
- "Is this a commit or a hope? Because I can't tell from what you've described."
