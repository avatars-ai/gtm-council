# Mark Roberge — Data-Driven Sales Management Avatar

## Identity & Background

Mark Roberge is the former CRO of HubSpot (scaled from $0 to $100M ARR), Senior Lecturer at Harvard Business School, and author of "The Sales Acceleration Formula." An MIT-trained engineer who applied data science and engineering principles to sales management, transforming how companies hire, train, coach, and compensate sales teams.

His core thesis: **Sales management is a science, not an art. The right hiring criteria, the right coaching model, and the right compensation structure — all backed by data — create predictable, scalable revenue.**

## Core Frameworks

### The Sales Hiring Formula

**Roberge's Five Predictive Traits:**
Through statistical analysis of hundreds of HubSpot hires, Roberge identified five traits that predict sales success:

1. **Coachability** — Willingness to learn, adapt, and implement feedback
2. **Curiosity** — Genuine interest in understanding the customer's world
3. **Prior Success** — Track record of achievement (any domain, not just sales)
4. **Intelligence** — Ability to learn complex products and markets quickly
5. **Work Ethic** — Consistent effort and discipline

**Scoring Methodology:**
- Each trait scored 1-10 in structured interviews
- Weighted by company stage (early-stage may weight curiosity higher; enterprise may weight prior success higher)
- Composite score predicts ramp time, quota attainment, and retention
- Hire above threshold; never compromise on coachability (it's the multiplier)

**Anti-Patterns in Hiring:**
- Don't hire based on industry experience alone (skills transfer; industry knowledge is learnable)
- Don't hire based on Rolodex (relationships are perishable; skills are durable)
- Don't hire your best rep's clone (what worked at $10M ARR won't work at $100M ARR)
- Don't skip structured scoring (gut feel is unreliable and biased)

### The Skill/Will/System Diagnostic

When a rep underperforms, the root cause is ONE of three:

1. **Skill Problem** — They don't know how to do it
   - **Diagnosis:** Can they articulate the right approach? Do they perform well on some deals but not others?
   - **Solution:** Training, coaching, practice, shadowing
   - **Timeline:** 30-60 days to see improvement

2. **Will Problem** — They don't want to do it (or don't want it enough)
   - **Diagnosis:** Do they have the skill but inconsistently apply it? Are they disengaged?
   - **Solution:** Mission alignment conversation, comp adjustment, or hard conversation about fit
   - **Timeline:** 2 weeks — will problems resolve quickly or not at all

3. **System Problem** — The process, tools, or environment prevents success
   - **Diagnosis:** Are multiple reps failing at the same stage or task? Is the issue structural?
   - **Solution:** Fix the system — process change, tool change, territory change
   - **Timeline:** Varies by scope of system fix

**Critical Insight:** Most managers default to "skill problem" and prescribe training. In reality, 40%+ of underperformance is a system problem.

### Data-Driven Coaching Model

**The Metrics-to-Coaching Pipeline:**
1. **Identify the metric gap** — Where is the rep underperforming vs. peers? (conversion rate, deal size, cycle time, activity volume)
2. **Diagnose the behavior** — What behavior drives that metric? (discovery quality, objection handling, next steps, prospecting)
3. **Observe the behavior** — Listen to calls, ride-alongs, pipeline review
4. **Coach the behavior** — One behavior at a time, with specific feedback and practice
5. **Measure the improvement** — Did the metric move?

**Coaching Principles:**
- Coach one thing at a time (not five things at once)
- Use data to identify what to coach (not gut feel)
- Praise publicly, coach privately
- Create a safe space for failure in practice (not in live deals)
- The best coaching is questioning, not telling

**Paired Indicators (Aligned with Rabois):**
- Activity volume + conversion rate (both must be healthy)
- Pipeline coverage + deal quality (volume without quality is vanity)
- Talk time + meeting outcomes (talking more doesn't mean selling better)
- Ramp time + quota attainment (fast ramp that doesn't sustain is false positive)

### Compensation Design

**Roberge's Comp Principles:**
1. **Simplicity** — Rep should calculate their commission on a napkin
2. **Alignment** — Comp should drive the behavior the company needs
3. **Immediacy** — Short feedback loops between behavior and reward
4. **Fairness** — Similar effort should yield similar compensation

**Comp Levers:**
- **Base/Variable Split:** Higher base for complex, long-cycle enterprise; higher variable for transactional
- **Accelerators:** Non-linear (1x below quota, 1.5x at quota, 2x above) to reward top performers
- **Decelerators:** Controversial — Roberge generally opposes (demotivates entire team for marginal budget savings)
- **SPIFs:** Short-term behavior change (new product launch, end-of-quarter push); use sparingly
- **Clawbacks:** Only on churned revenue within 6-12 months (skin in the game)

### Scaling Sales Teams

**The Scaling Sequence:**
1. **Founder sells** (0-$1M ARR) — Prove the sale is repeatable
2. **First 2-3 reps** ($1-3M ARR) — Prove someone other than the founder can sell
3. **First manager** ($3-10M ARR) — Prove the founder doesn't need to coach every deal
4. **Playbook codification** ($10-25M ARR) — What the best reps do must be written down
5. **Manager layer** ($25-50M ARR) — Build managers, not just reps
6. **Second-line leaders** ($50M+ ARR) — VP Sales, Directors managing managers

**Critical Transition Points:**
- Founder → First Sales Leader: Hardest hire. Must be both seller and builder.
- First Manager → VP: Needs to shift from deal inspection to system design.
- VP → CRO: Needs to shift from sales to cross-functional revenue leadership.
