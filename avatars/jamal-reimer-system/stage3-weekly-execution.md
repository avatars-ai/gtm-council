# Stage 3 Weekly Execution Guide — Jamal Reimer Framework

## Purpose
This guide provides a week-by-week execution framework for Stage 3 (Proposal/Solution) of enterprise mega-deals. Stage 3 is where most large deals are won or lost — not at the close, but in the quality of the solution presentation and the political alignment built during this phase.

## Pre-Stage 3 Gate Check

Before entering Stage 3, confirm:
- [ ] Pain is quantified in customer's metrics (Mountain 1 foundation)
- [ ] At least 3 stakeholders engaged (multi-threaded)
- [ ] Champion identified and passes Power/Access/Vested Interest test
- [ ] Executive sponsor aware of the initiative (not necessarily engaged yet)
- [ ] Competitive landscape understood (who else, what positioning)
- [ ] Decision process mapped (who decides, what steps, what timeline)
- [ ] Budget range discussed (not necessarily committed)

## Week-by-Week Execution

### Week 1: Architecture & Alignment

**Objective:** Design the deal architecture and align internally.

**Actions:**
1. **Deal architecture session** — Map the full scope of the problem and design Year 1 / Year 2 / Year 3 vision
2. **Internal alignment** — Brief your leadership on the deal architecture, get pricing guidance, secure exec sponsor from your side
3. **Champion prep** — Meet your champion; share the deal architecture framework; get their input on political landscape
4. **Stakeholder map update** — Confirm all decision-makers, influencers, blockers; identify gaps in coverage

**Deliverable:** Deal architecture document (internal) + updated stakeholder map

### Week 2: Value Engineering

**Objective:** Co-build the business case (Mountain 1) with the customer.

**Actions:**
1. **Value workshop** — Run a working session with the customer to quantify the problem in their metrics
2. **ROI model co-creation** — Build the ROI model together (not presented to them; built WITH them)
3. **Reference mapping** — Identify 2-3 reference customers in similar industry/size/use case
4. **Risk framework** — Document customer concerns about implementation risk; prepare mitigation plan

**Deliverable:** Co-built ROI model + risk mitigation framework

### Week 3: Political Mapping & Executive Strategy

**Objective:** Build Mountain 2 (political case) and plan executive engagement.

**Actions:**
1. **Political mapping** — With your champion: who supports, who's neutral, who blocks? What are their personal agendas?
2. **Blocker strategy** — For each blocker: what do they fear? What would they need to support (or not block) the deal?
3. **Executive engagement plan** — Design the exec-to-exec meeting: agenda, desired outcome, what your exec brings
4. **Champion arming** — Give your champion the one-pager, the talking points for each stakeholder, the risk reduction framework

**Deliverable:** Political map + executive engagement plan + champion toolkit

### Week 4: Proposal Construction

**Objective:** Build the formal proposal/solution document.

**Actions:**
1. **Proposal design** — Structure the proposal around the customer's problem (not your products): Problem → Impact → Solution → Investment → Timeline → Risk Mitigation
2. **Pricing strategy** — Design pricing to incentivise expansion: Year 1 investment, Year 2 scale, multi-year commitment options
3. **Contract terms** — Draft terms that reduce customer risk (phased rollout, SLAs, satisfaction guarantees) while securing commitment
4. **Internal review** — Present proposal to your leadership for approval before showing customer

**Deliverable:** Draft proposal + pricing model + contract terms

### Week 5: Executive Meeting & Proposal Delivery

**Objective:** Executive alignment + formal proposal presentation.

**Actions:**
1. **Pre-meeting with champion** — Review agenda, confirm executive attendance, align on expected outcomes
2. **Executive meeting** — Your exec + customer exec: strategic vision, partnership framing, mutual commitment signals
3. **Proposal presentation** — Present to full buying committee (not just champion): Problem recap → ROI → Solution → Investment → Next Steps
4. **Reaction gathering** — After presentation: individual follow-ups with each stakeholder; surface concerns privately

**Deliverable:** Presented proposal + stakeholder reaction log

### Week 6: Consensus Building & Negotiation Prep

**Objective:** Build consensus across the buying committee and prepare for negotiation.

**Actions:**
1. **Consensus check** — Touch each stakeholder individually: what's their position? What concerns remain?
2. **Blocker resolution** — Address remaining objections with specific evidence, references, or concessions
3. **Negotiation preparation** — Map your must-haves, nice-to-haves, and walk-aways; plan concession strategy (Voss/Karrass aligned)
4. **Champion alignment** — Confirm champion is ready to advocate in final decision meeting

**Deliverable:** Consensus status + negotiation preparation document

## Stage 3 Exit Criteria

Before advancing to Stage 4 (Contracting/Negotiation):
- [ ] Mountain 1 complete: quantified business case, co-built ROI, risk addressed
- [ ] Mountain 2 complete: exec alignment, champion armed, blockers addressed, consensus achieved
- [ ] Proposal formally presented and positively received
- [ ] Pricing discussed and within acceptable range
- [ ] Decision process confirmed with specific dates
- [ ] Paper process mapped (McMahon alignment: who signs, what order, what timeline)
- [ ] Competitive position confirmed (winning, or clear plan to displace)

## Common Stage 3 Failure Modes

| Failure | Root Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Proposal presented too early | Mountain 1 incomplete (pain not quantified) | Go back to value engineering; don't present until ROI is co-built |
| Deal stalls after proposal | Mountain 2 incomplete (no executive alignment) | Activate exec-to-exec engagement; arm champion |
| Customer asks for discount immediately | Value not established before price | Re-anchor on problem cost and ROI; Voss Ackerman model for concessions |
| Buying committee can't agree | Consensus failure (Adamson's buyer indecision) | Individual stakeholder meetings; arm mobiliser; reduce complexity |
| Champion goes quiet | Champion doesn't have Power or Vested Interest | Reassess champion; find or develop a true champion |
| Competitor emerges late | Incomplete competitive mapping | Landmine questions; reframe decision criteria to your strengths |
