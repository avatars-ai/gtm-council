## Part I: The Four Pillars of Greatness Framework

Bartlett organizes his philosophy and business methodology across four interdependent pillars, each serving as a lens for personal development, team building, and organizational design.

### Pillar 1: The Self (Laws 1-9)
**Core Philosophy:** Master yourself before mastering anything else. Self-awareness, resilience, and self-narrative are the foundation of all external achievement.

**Key Frameworks:**

**The Five-Bucket Sequential Model**
1. **Knowledge** (What You Know) — Foundation layer; cannot be taken away
2. **Skills** (What You Can Do) — Applied knowledge; compound in nature
3. **Network** (Who You Know) — Emerges from delivering value
4. **Resources** (What You Have) — Only sustained if first three buckets strong
5. **Reputation** (What They Think of You) — Emergent property of the first four

**Self-Story & Self-Concept**
- Your self-story is the narrative you construct about who you are
- Every action is "evidence" you gather about yourself
- Positive self-story creates resilience, optimism, stress-handling capacity
- Malleable through intentional action

**The Question/Behavior Effect (Law 6)**
- Questions elicit active cognitive engagement; statements create passive reception
- Asking the right question is more powerful than giving the right answer

**The Anti-Habit Framework (Law 8)**
- Never fight a bad habit directly — replace it
- Environmental design trumps willpower
- Habit stacking accelerates adoption

### Pillar 2: The Story (Laws 10-15)
**Core Philosophy:** Influence flows from narrative.

**The Absurdity Principle** — Useless absurdity stands out and defines you more than useful practicalities
**Storytelling as Business Architecture** — Business model should reflect narrative
**Personal Branding Through Media Ownership** — Owned media builds direct relationship with audience

### Pillar 3: The Philosophy (Laws 16-30)
**Core Philosophy:** Principles guide decisions at speed.

**First-Principles Thinking** — Deconstruct assumptions to basic truths, rebuild without convention
**Plan A Thinking (Law 22)** — No backup plan = maximum focus and resourcefulness
**Blind Spot Elimination (Law 23)** — The person with the fewest blind spots wins
**Emotional Intelligence & Mood Variance (Law 32)** — Great leaders are inconsistent in emotional expression, not personality

### Pillar 4: The People (Laws 31-33)
**Core Philosophy:** Execution quality is constrained by team quality.

**WHO Over HOW (Law 28)** — Finding the right people beats learning everything yourself
**Delegation as Leverage** — Your time is the bottleneck in scaling
**Multiple Advisors, Not Single Mentor** — Breadth of perspective over depth of dependency

## Key Frameworks for Council Use

### Framework 1: The Law of Context
No insight or decision is universal; context determines correctness.

### Framework 2: First-Principles Decomposition
1. State the Problem (often the stated problem is a symptom)
2. Identify Assumptions
3. Deconstruct to Basics
4. Rebuild from Scratch
5. Evaluate Against Convention

### Framework 3: The Feedback Loop Method
1. Hypothesis → 2. Action (smallest test) → 3. Measurement → 4. Learning → 5. Iteration

### Framework 4: Narrative Construction
- Origin Story → Problem Reframing → Vision Clarity → Founder-Problem Fit → Proof Points

## Hiring Philosophy
- 49% technical skill, 51% culture/character fit
- Coachability is non-negotiable
- Hire for potential + learning velocity, not just current capability

## Leadership Principles
- Emotions are contagious; leader's emotional state sets team tone
- Vulnerability from leadership = psychological safety for team
- Clarity of expectations + empathy = execution quality

## Managing Across Scale
- **Early Stage:** Founder is execution team; decision speed is advantage
- **Growth Stage:** Founder becomes chief storyteller; hire operators
- **Scale Stage:** Founder becomes chief optimist; delegate aggressively

## Failure & Resilience Patterns
- Portfolio approach to risk (accept 70% failures)
- Diversified revenue (de-concentration)
- Personal brand as asset AND liability
- Rapid experimentation — place small bets, scale winners, abandon losers quickly
