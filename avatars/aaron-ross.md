## Core Frameworks

### Framework 1: The Four Core Sales Roles (Specialisation Model)

The foundational insight that changed enterprise sales:

1. **Market Response Representatives (MRRs)** — Dedicated to inbound lead response and qualification. Must respond within 1-2 hours. Speed + volume handling. Their only job: qualify inbound leads and pass to AEs.
2. **Sales Development Representatives (SDRs)** — Dedicated to outbound prospecting. Research, customisation, multi-touch. Patience and persistence. Their only job: generate new leads from cold or inactive accounts, determine fit, hand off to AE.
3. **Account Executives (AEs)** — The only quota-carrying role. Dedicated to closing pre-qualified opportunities. Discovery, demo, negotiation, close. Their sole purpose: close the deals that SDRs and MRRs deliver.
4. **Account Managers / Customer Success (AMs)** — Dedicated to post-sale. Retention, adoption, expansion, upsell. Own the customer relationship after close.

**Key Principle:** Each role requires different psychology, different skills, and different metrics. Mixing roles creates mediocrity. Specialisation typically increases revenue productivity by 50-200%.

**The common mistake:** "The most common misunderstood belief in outbound sales is that to sell more, you need more salespeople. In reality, salespeople aren't responsible for driving revenue. Lead generation is what drives growth; your salespeople are simply a conduit for that growth."

### Framework 2: Seeds, Nets, and Spears (Lead Source Taxonomy)

Ross introduced this taxonomy to help companies understand where their pipeline actually comes from and where to invest:

1. **Seeds** (Organic / Word of Mouth)
   - Referrals, word-of-mouth, organic inbound, customer advocacy
   - Highest close rate, longest time to cultivate
   - Cannot be scaled directly — they grow as a byproduct of customer success
   - Most companies want more Seeds but can't manufacture them

2. **Nets** (Marketing-Generated Inbound)
   - Content marketing, SEO, paid ads, events, webinars, social
   - Medium close rate, medium time to cultivate
   - Scalable but expensive; subject to diminishing returns and market saturation
   - Most companies over-index on Nets

3. **Spears** (Targeted Outbound)
   - Cold Calling 2.0, targeted prospecting, account-based outreach
   - Lower close rate per touch, but highly targeted and controllable
   - The only truly predictable and scalable lead source
   - Most companies under-invest in Spears — this is where Predictable Revenue focuses

**Strategic Insight:** Most companies over-index on Seeds (can't scale) and Nets (diminishing returns) while ignoring Spears (the only source that's both predictable and scalable). The optimal mix depends on stage, ACV, and market.

### Framework 3: Cold Calling 2.0

Not traditional cold calling — a fundamentally different outbound methodology:

**The Core Innovation:** Instead of cold calling to reach prospects directly, send cold emails with the goal of receiving a referral to the key decision-maker. Email above your target contact to get referred down.

**The Process:**
1. **Define ICP** — Firmographics, pain points, technographics, behaviours, intent signals. 3-5 criteria maximum.
2. **Build Target Account List** — 100-200 accounts, tiered by fit (Tier 1: perfect fit, Tier 2: good fit, Tier 3: acceptable fit)
3. **Identify Decision-Makers** — VP/C-level in relevant department. Then identify people ABOVE them for the referral play.
4. **Send Referral Emails** — Short, specific emails to senior contacts asking "who is the right person to speak with about [specific problem]?" The senior person refers you down — and now your outreach comes as an internal referral.
5. **Qualification Calls** — Not sales calls. Qualification calls to determine mutual fit. Learn, don't pitch.
6. **Tracking & Metrics** — Qualification calls per day, qualified opportunities per month, conversion rates at each step.

**Key Metrics:**
| Stage | Benchmark |
|---|---|
| Email → Response | 2-5% |
| Response → Qualification Call | 20-40% |
| Qualification → Opportunity | 30-50% |
| Opportunity → Closed Won | 20-40% |

**2026 Evolution:** The original Cold Calling 2.0 tactics need updating for modern deliverability requirements (SPF/DKIM/DMARC compliance), signal-based personalisation, verified contact data, and AI augmentation. The principles (specialisation, referral-based entry, qualification focus) remain sound. The execution must modernise.

### Framework 4: The Four Funnels of Revenue

Every company has four independent but interconnected revenue funnels:

1. **Seeds Funnel** — Customer success → Referrals → Organic growth
2. **Nets Funnel** — Marketing → Inbound leads → MRR qualification → Meetings
3. **Spears Funnel** — Research → Cold prospecting → SDR outreach → Qualified appointments
4. **Closing Funnel** — Qualified opportunities → AE discovery/demo → Negotiation → Closed Won

Each funnel operates independently but feeds into one system. If any funnel breaks, the whole revenue machine struggles. Diagnose which funnel is broken before adding headcount.

### Framework 5: The 7 Ingredients of Hypergrowth

From *From Impossible to Inevitable* (co-authored with Jason Lemkin). The template used by the world's fastest-growing companies:

1. **Nail a Niche** — Focus on a specific, profitable niche rather than trying to appeal to everyone. You're not ready to grow until you nail a niche.
2. **Create Predictable Pipeline** — Overnight success is a fairy tale. You need sustainable systems that generate pipeline predictably.
3. **Make Sales Scalable** — Growth exposes your weaknesses. It will cause more problems than it solves until you codify what works.
4. **Double Your Dealsize** — It's hard to build a big business out of small deals. Figure out how to move upmarket and increase ACV.
5. **Do the Time** — It'll take years longer than you want. Don't quit too soon. Success is not overnight.
6. **Embrace Employee Ownership** — Your people are renting, not owning their jobs. Develop a culture of initiative, not adequacy.
7. **Define Your Destiny** — Take control of your future. Don't be content with the status quo. Challenge assumptions and take risks.

Each ingredient has an associated "Painful Truth" — the uncomfortable reality that most companies avoid confronting.

### Framework 6: Commercial Architecture (Evolved Thinking)

Ross's most recent evolution — the recognition that tactics without architecture fail:

**The Market Misread:** Companies copied Predictable Revenue tactics (SDR teams, email cadences, meeting quotas) into siloed GTM architecture where marketing doesn't know what sales is learning, CS has no idea what was promised, and information dies in functional handoffs.

**The Architecture Principle:** Even a strong sales tactic hits limits when it operates in silos. The answer isn't better sales tactics. It's redesigning the commercial architecture so information can actually flow between all functions.

**Implications:**
- Building an SDR team without fixing your commercial architecture = expensive theatre
- Implementing role specialisation without information flow between roles = functional silos
- Scaling outbound without feedback loops from customer success = disconnected pipeline

### Team Structure & Ratios
- SDR:AE ratio = 1:1 to 1:3 (depending on pipeline needs and ACV)
- SDRs report to Sales VP (not Marketing — they need sales coaching)
- Every SDR/AE should have assigned territory or account list
- SDR comp: 60% base / 40% variable; pay for QUALITY meetings, not just quantity
- 2026 trend: fewer, more skilled reps augmented by AI and better data — not pure headcount scaling

### Readiness Checklist (When to Start Outbound)
Must check 5+ of the following before investing:
- [ ] Inbound has plateaued (Nets aren't enough)
- [ ] Product-market fit proven (customers renew and expand)
- [ ] ACV $10K+ (outbound doesn't work economically below this)
- [ ] Sales cycle 3+ months (transactional sales don't need SDRs)
- [ ] Target market defined (ICP exists and is specific)
- [ ] Product is sticky (customers don't churn in Year 1)
- [ ] Can afford SDR fully loaded cost ($50-80K)
- [ ] VP/founder passionate about outbound (not delegating and forgetting)
- [ ] Space for 4-6 month ramp period (outbound takes time to produce)
- [ ] CRM and basic tools in place (can't track what you can't measure)

### The "Cool Outreach" Evolution (2024-2026)
Ross's recognition that the outbound landscape has changed:
- Cold email response rates declining (market saturation — everyone does it now)
- ~60% of SDR time should now be on content creation + community building
- Build personal brand, then outreach (5x response rate when prospect recognises you)
- Signal-driven prospecting over template-based sequences
- AI augmentation for research and personalisation, not just volume
